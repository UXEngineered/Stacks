# Stacks Principles

> A lineage-first system for how teams think, decide, and evolve work in the AI era

---

## Core Design Choice

**Stacks does not ask teams to do their work inside a new tool. It brings shared lineage to where work is already happening.**

Rather than acting as a central hub that replaces existing tools, Stacks operates as an orchestration layer that quietly observes, captures, and connects work across the systems teams already use.

---

## Foundational Concepts

### Fieldbooks
- Long-lived, forkable lineage graphs
- Contain artifacts, inputs, decisions, assumptions, and outcomes
- Preserve relationships: `derived from`, `informed by`, `superseded`, `resulted in`
- **Fieldbooks are not phases or workspaces. They are records of evolution.**

### Stacks
- The application and ecosystem layer
- Hosts multiple Fieldbooks
- Manages roles, access, and visibility
- Renders purpose-driven views (sales, discovery, architecture, client, executive)
- **Stacks changes emphasis, not truth.**

---

## Core Thesis

> The future of work is not better collaboration. It is better preservation of intent.

In an AI-accelerated world:
- Artifacts are cheap
- Drafts are infinite
- Outputs are abundant

**What becomes scarce is why something exists, what informed it, what changed, and what depends on it now.**

---

## The Problem We Solve

Teams do not fail due to lack of documentation. **They fail due to context collapse over time.**

Common symptoms:
- Sales knowledge disappears at handoff
- Discovery decisions lose rationale within weeks
- Architecture choices outlive their assumptions
- Clients inherit conclusions without lineage
- Executives see outputs but not intent

Existing tools optimize for creation, coordination, or storage. **None optimize for shared lineage.**

---

## The Invariant

> Artifacts, tools, and workflows will always change. **Lineage is the invariant.**

Stacks exists to ensure that:
- Work does not lose its "why"
- Discovery is continuous, not phased
- AI amplifies understanding rather than erasing context
- Organizations retain institutional memory without slowing down

---

## What We Are NOT

Stacks is explicitly **not**:
- Project management
- Task or sprint tracking
- Real-time collaboration (Google-style)
- A document editor or whiteboard replacement
- A Jira, Asana, or Notion competitor

**If a feature helps control work, it is out of scope. If it helps understand work, it is aligned.**

---

## Features We Intentionally Do Not Build

- Named phases or gates
- Status indicators (on track, at risk)
- Backlogs, tickets, or sprints
- Heavy integrations with delivery tooling
- Prescriptive workflows

---

## AI Guardrails

1. **AI proposes, never commits** — Humans confirm, supersede, or reject
2. **All synthesis is cited** — Every AI-generated insight traces back to source material
3. **AI amplifies understanding** — It does not replace human judgment or erase context

---

## Tool-Agnostic by Design

- External links are first-class citizens
- Durable snapshots preserve meaning beyond tool access
- AI operates on representations, not live tools

This ensures **lineage survives tool variance and permission rot**.

---

## Strategic Guardrail

> Does this reduce the cost of preserving intent, or does it increase the cost of doing the work?

**Only the first belongs in Stacks.**

---

## Key Principles Summary

| Principle | Implication |
|-----------|-------------|
| Lineage-first | Relationships between artifacts matter more than the artifacts themselves |
| Passive capture | Reduce documentation tax through AI-proposed lineage with human confirmation |
| Tool-agnostic | Respect external tools, preserve snapshots, don't force migration |
| Evolution over status | Reveal how work evolved, not where it stands in a process |
| Intent preservation | The "why" is as important as the "what" |
| Human authority | AI assists, humans decide |

---

## Closing Position

> Stacks is not the next productivity product. It is infrastructure for how modern, AI-accelerated teams reason over time.

We are not building for how teams worked. **We are building for how teams will work.**
